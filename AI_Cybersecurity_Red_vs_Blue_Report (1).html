Overview

Executive Summary

The cybersecurity landscape is experiencing its most profound transformation since the advent of the internet.

Artificial Intelligence is no longer a peripheral tool—it has become the central battlefield where offensive and defensive capabilities collide. This research synthesizes insights from the world's foremost cybersecurity professionals, analyzing how AI is reshaping both red team (offensive) and blue team (defensive) operations in 2025-2026.

The consensus among top-tier professionals is clear: we have entered the "Agentic AI Era" where autonomous systems don't just assist human operators—they execute complex attack and defense workflows at machine speed. The traditional boundaries between red and blue teams are blurring as both sides leverage identical AI infrastructure, creating an unprecedented arms race where the winner is determined by who can best orchestrate human expertise with artificial intelligence.

He demonstrates multimodal backdooring of PDFs, images, metadata, and QR codes with JavaScript attacks that execute when processed by LLM systems.

Convergence

Cross-Cutting Themes

Where red and blue operations converge in the AI era.

01

The Agentic AI Paradigm

Both red and blue teams are moving beyond chatbots to autonomous agents that plan, execute, and adapt without continuous human oversight. The same underlying technology powers both offense and defense.

02

The Speed Asymmetry Problem

Red teams historically had the advantage of time. AI compresses this to minutes. Blue teams must now achieve machine-speed defense or accept perpetual disadvantage.

03

The Expertise Encoding Challenge

The most advanced practitioners emphasize that raw AI models cannot replace human expertise—they must be fine-tuned, tooled, and guided by decades of operational experience.

04

The Identity Crisis

AI agents require identities, permissions, and access controls. Both red and blue teams are grappling with non-human identity management as a core security discipline.

05

The Prompt Injection Universal Threat

Jason Haddix's research and Heather Adkins's warnings converge on prompt injection as the fundamental vulnerability of the AI era—affecting both offensive exploitation and defensive integrity.

Strategic Guidance

Recommendations

Strategic guidance for navigating the AI transformation in cybersecurity.

🔴 For Red Team Professionals

  • Evolve from tool users to AI orchestrators. The future belongs to those who can encode their expertise into autonomous agents, not those who merely use AI-generated scripts.
  • Master prompt injection at depth. Jason Haddix's taxonomy approach should be standard training—understanding 60+ categories of injection techniques is now baseline knowledge.
  • Develop AI ecosystem thinking. Attack the entire AI pipeline (data, model, prompt, application, connected APIs) rather than focusing on single components.
  • Embrace continuous testing. Kevin Mandia's model of 24/7 autonomous red teaming will become the industry standard—annual pentests are obsolete.

🔵 For Blue Team Professionals

  • Shift to machine-speed defense. If your SOC still relies on human triage for every alert, you are operationally defeated. Invest in autonomous SOC capabilities immediately.
  • Implement AI agent governance now. With 40% of enterprise applications expected to include AI agents by end of 2026 (Gartner), agent inventory and permission auditing is critical.
  • Adopt the "assume breach" mindset. Heather Adkins's approach—focusing on dwell time minimization rather than breach prevention—is the realistic posture for AI-era defense.
  • Invest in specialized AI models. General LLMs are insufficient for security operations. Deploy domain-specific models for threat detection, log analysis, and vulnerability assessment.

⚡ For Security Leaders

  • Budget for AI-native security. EY research shows 85% of security leaders believe current budgets are insufficient for AI-enabled threats. The investment gap is a strategic vulnerability.
  • Build human-AI hybrid teams. The most effective model combines human strategic thinking with AI tactical execution. Neither alone is sufficient.
  • Address the talent crisis. 90% of leaders struggle to recruit AI-security professionals. Internal upskilling programs are not optional—they are survival mechanisms.
  • Demand decision-grade proof. Whether from red team or blue team tools, executives need clear evidence of exploitable risk or defensive effectiveness—not theoretical metrics.

🌎 For the Cybersecurity Community

  • Share AI attack research responsibly. The balance between disclosure and protection is shifting—coordinate with vendors and platforms to fix AI vulnerabilities before widespread exploitation.
  • Develop AI security standards. The industry needs common frameworks for AI red teaming, agent security, and autonomous defense evaluation.
  • Mentor the next generation. As AI democratizes attack capabilities, we must equally democratize defensive knowledge. The professionals entering the field today will face challenges we cannot yet imagine.

Conclusion

The AI transformation in cybersecurity is not coming—it is here. The professionals profiled in this research represent the vanguard of a fundamental shift in how we approach both offense and defense. Their collective insight reveals a clear trajectory: autonomous, agentic AI systems will dominate cybersecurity operations within the next 2-3 years.

The question is no longer whether AI will change cybersecurity, but whether organizations can adapt their strategies, talent, and technologies fast enough to survive the transition. The red team has historically held the initiative in cybersecurity. AI threatens to make this advantage permanent unless blue teams achieve machine-speed defense capabilities.

For those of us in the cybersecurity profession, this is our defining moment. The tools we build, the knowledge we share, and the standards we establish today will determine whether the AI era becomes a golden age of security or its darkest chapter.

The leaders featured in this research have shown us the path.
It is now our responsibility to walk it.
